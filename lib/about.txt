dbunkr is an open platform for turning public curiosity into funded research. Instead of approaching fact checking like a strictly binary problem, this site tries to organize/map the debate. Areas where there is more disagreement can then be formulated into testable hypotheses, and can receive crowdfunding for researchers who can bid to work on them. 

Every question on dbunkr has a "river" -  a communal, live conversation with Claude and Grok (and maybe more LLM’s soon!) running side by side. Your questions get answered by the llms and (after a little bit of moderation) get added to the questions “canon” - shaping the conversation for all others after. The goal is to change the existing idea of a single person talking to a single LLM, and instead treat everyone as contributing to a shared record that everyone can read, react to, and learn from.

Anyone can add new questions and put them up for discussion. Interested parties can contribute information by adding it to the canon. When a question is first introduced, it is marked as “open for funding”. If enough people vote to close it, it will become “established” and will not be available to receive funding any longer. 

Every week, the research question that is rated as most important will receive the entire communal research “pot”. To receive this money, individuals or groups with a ORCID can “bid” on a question, offering to research it in return for the pot. Anyone can vote to raise the importance of the question, or vote for or against specific bids. Some bids on some long-running questions might get multiple rounds of “winning the pot”, as we make slow progress on some of societies’ toughest problems.

Funding for the site (really just llm calls right now) comes from a separate communal pot. When it runs out, the LLM’s will stop working, and wait for the generosity of other user’s to continue. Modern day take-a-penny-leave-a-penny!

dbunkr is open source and just getting started. I wanted to try to close the gap between what people wonder and what researchers study. I’m very open to feedback - write me at sodikroehler.bsky.social with any thoughts!
