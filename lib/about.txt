**dbunkr** is an open platform for turning public curiosity about misinformation into funded research. Search any claim — vaccines, elections, conspiracy theories — and find out what the scientific consensus actually says, how people across the political spectrum think about it, and what questions remain genuinely unanswered.

Every claim on dbunkr has a "river" — a communal, live conversation with Claude and Grok running side by side. When you ask a question, you're not talking to an AI alone. You're contributing to a shared record that everyone can read, and the best answers become permanent canon for that topic.

When a question can't be answered — not because it's complex, but because the research genuinely doesn't exist yet — it becomes an open research posting. Anyone can fund it. Researchers bid on it. The community votes on which bid to fund, and when a researcher produces work the community accepts as closing the question, they get paid.

The pot funds API calls for everyone. When it runs low, contributions keep the rivers running. Separately, funded research postings are paid out when the community votes to accept a closing document. No single person decides what counts as answered — the community does.

dbunkr is open source and early. The code is MIT licensed. The mission is simple: the gap between what people wonder and what researchers study shouldn't exist. We're building the infrastructure to close it.
