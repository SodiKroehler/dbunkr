dbunkr is an open platform for turning public curiosity about misinformation into funded research. Instead of approaching fact checking like a strictly binary problem, this site tries to organize/map the debate. Research where there is more disagreement can then be formulated into testable hypothesis, and can receive crowdfunding for them. 

Every question on dbunkr has a "river" — a communal, live conversation with Claude and Grok running side by side. Your questions get answered, and if they are substantially different from other’s questions, get added to the questions “canon” - shaping the conversation for others. While becoming educated you’re also contributing to a shared record that everyone can read, react to, and learn from.

When a question is significantly different that it can’t fit under any existing questions, it’s entered in as a new “novel” question. Interested parties can contribute information, or add things to it’s canon as with any other question. However, it is also marked as “open for funding”. If enough people vote to close it, it will become “established”, and not open for funding. 

Every week, the top rated research question will receive the entire communal research “pot”. To receive this money, individuals or groups with a ORCID can “bid” on a question, offering to research it in return for the pot. Anyone can vote to raise the importance of the question, or vote for or against specific bids. Once the the funding has been earmarked, that specific ORCID must submit a paper or significant contribution which causes at least two people to vote to mark the issue “closed”, which disburses the payment.

Funding for the site (really just token calls) comes from a separate communal pot. When it runs low, contributions keep the rivers running. Separately, funded research postings are paid out when the community votes to accept a closing document. No single person decides what counts as answered — the community does.

dbunkr is open source and just getting started. I wanted to try to close the gap between what people wonder and what researchers study. I’m very open to feedback - write me at sodikroehler.bsky.social with any thoughts!
